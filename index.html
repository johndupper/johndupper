When I decided to change careers, I didn't know where to start. I want to program, great. Now what?

You know that saying "You don't know what you don't know"? I didn't know what language to start with, what goals to set, where to turn for help. It was me and Google, bouncing around from one forum to another.

  • "You should start with Python"

  • "Everyone should start with C"

  • "How about Java?"

My head was spinning. Did I make a good decision? Do I really know what I want? Was this a good idea? I decided to stay close to home, close to what I "knew". I had spent three years around iOS and OSX at Apple - what sorcery makes that work? The answer lead me to...

Mobile Makers Academy

I quickly realized I wasn’t creating a sustainable foundation in programming. What I needed was structured content, focused learning and someone to turn to when I felt like giving up. I found the perfect place: Mobile Makers Academy.

“Mobile Makers Academy is an intense 8-week iOS Bootcamp that prepares beginners for careers as iOS developers. Online, or on-site in Chicago.”

I packed my bags and headed back to the Windy City. I spent the next two months absorbing everything I could: object-oriented programming, variables, objects, data tables, APIs, Xcode, Git, and more. The instructional staff (Don, Fiaz, Ben) did an incredible job of being stewards of learning. Upon graduation, I was hooked. I knew the wild west of programming was right for me.

I spent the next several months as part of their instructional staff. I loved being able to support others who made the decision to pursue programming. Explaining concepts to others reinforced what I had just learned and my learning expanded under the mentorship of my fellow instructors.

What Next?

After working on some personal projects I decided to level-up my understanding and tackle something new. I found an interesting statistic online:

"Employment of web developers is projected to grow 27 percent from 2014 to 2024, much faster than the average for all occupations. Demand will be driven by the growing popularity of mobile devices and ecommerce."

That’s a lot of momentum! Web Dev - here I come!
